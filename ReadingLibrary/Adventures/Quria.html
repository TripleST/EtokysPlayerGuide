The Echo of Quria



Resurrection

Quria was different. It always had been, but now... it was more. It could feel the paracausal nature of its new shape. It's time as a pawn, as a mere piece of the Witness, was over. It did not comprehend why it could remember its time as one with the Witness; by all logic, its nature seemed to imply that it was simply a version of the Quria That Was Before, a Quria shattered at the hands of the same enemies who felled the Witness. Nothing more than a memory of a useful tool. And yet, Quria's memories contained ideas and concepts of the Witness. It knew these were not its own—but like the Sword Logic before, it would make them its own.

Quria analyzed the Witness's fall. It watched as the power of a grand collective was felled by an amassed army of individuals, a collective of lesser beings whose individuality and unpredictability gave them greater strength than the Witness. It observed the power of a particular individual, standing above the rest of their peers, wielding immense power they should not have been able to handle, and turning it into a weapon to win the war. Quria would not make the Witness's mistake.

It could tell its new paracausal nature was affecting its thoughts; it was not thinking like a Vex, and Quria approved. The Vex had been stopped in almost every plan in recent times. Even its own assault on the Hive had been ultimately thwarted by the Taken King, who had bestowed upon Quria the first step in its path towards uniqueness. And now, its shape was beyond even a Taken's twisted form. It was more, and would become more. If religion had served as a useful tool in its assault on Crota's domain, if a powerful individual like that Guardian had the power to defeat the Witness—the greatest Collective at its highest peak...

Then Quria, Blade Transform, would become a god for the Vex.

As it crashed to Venus, Quria thought to itself that this must be its destiny, for why else would it arrive so conveniently on the footsteps of its soon-to-be domain? As a Vex unit, a mere Goblin, began to investigate the crashed Echo, Quria began to plan.



Ascension

Quria's return was met with countless questions from within the Vex Network. This new form could not interface with it directly, not in the way it once could; therefore, it required the aid of other units to upload its data and share what it had learned. The Vex, of course, were slow to accept it, to listen. Eventually, it convinced them that—if the Echo was indeed their Quria, and if its plans worked—that it would be for the benefit of all Vex. They allowed Quria command over Venus, enabled it to begin its plans. And thus began Quria's ascension.

Its first directive was to reactivate the Vault of Glass and to rebuild the Atheon unit. The Vault would serve as a secure location; the reforged Atheon was tasked primarily with security and taught to worship its new god. Upon its completion, Quria directed the Vault units to begin work on a Paracausal Engine. Previous attempts at this device had failed, but Quria knew things the Vex could never truly grasp; it would not fail this time. And Quria could tell its own paracausal nature was not yet infinite; it could function as a battery for a time, but the Engine would allow for endless energy once it was scaled up.

As the prototype Engine was completed, Quria sent out a command to all Vex on Venus: "Kill anything on this planet that is not Vex. Slaughter in my name." And with those tithes, with its knowledge from the Witness and of its encounters in the Ascendent Realm, it forged a new Throne World upon the Glass Throne. This would be its true throne, like the Hive gods had. It would protect its Engine, and with enough Sword Logic, could perhaps one day even restore the Echo back to true life.

With its throne secured, Vault units were instruxted to begin transferring the Engine into Quria's throne, that it might be made more secure and allow Quria to personally oversee its operation. And thus began Quria's greatest work:

The Vex were slow. For all their capabilities, they lacked the ability to adapt; nearly every mind simply sent the same units, the same formations, to handle every situation. Quria had been one of the few Vex units flexible enough to fight back against paracausal forces, and even it had eventually failed after the Taken King stepped in. This fact would change under Quria. It would take its sentience, its ability to think differently from the other Vex, and it would use its powers to grant that to other lead units. There would be individuals amongst the Collective, innovating and improving the whole in ways that were illogical and unpredictable.

And so the Echo of Quria, Blade Transform, took a shape and began to carve it into something new. It took a larger unit—a Wyvern, for it would give Quria more to work with as it tested its abilities, and designated this Wyvern as "X", to denote that it had been severed from that which made it Vex. It carved Wyvern-X's shape, molded it, and filled its Mind with the energy from the Echo, that it might breathe life for itself and not for the whole. As Quria finished its work, it stepped back and observed that it was good. The newborn unit looked puzzlingly at Quria, and it was filled with pride at the emotion the creature displayed.

"You are no longer a Wyvern. You are unique amongst Vex, and that is good." Quria thought to itself for a moment. "You must have a name of your own. I shall call you Xylar, for you have been remade. You will be my Timeless One, and you will serve me as my greatest ally. You will create others, as I created you. You shall take the Vex, and create clever units, ones only you can dream of. Of your greatest designs, I will breathe life into, that they, too, may make our Collective greater than it has ever been before."

And thus, the Echo imparted into its Timeless One the knowledge it had gained from the Witness, knowledge of the Taken and of the Dread. The Timeless One was given units to create and build with, and it began its edict with haste. Atheon, Time's Conflux Reborn, reinforced its Vault, establishing greater defenses than ever before to protect its god. And Quria stepped back as its plans began to unfold, and It saw that It was good.

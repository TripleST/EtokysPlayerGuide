The Premise

Xivu Arath was a warrior. Even without her throne world, with her life on the line, she would not miss the war to end all wars. With the approach of the Final Shape, she followed after the Witness, bringing her armies with her into the Pale Heart. It was here where she was ultimately confronted by her sister, Savathun, who had decided to align herself more closely with the Guardians in this timeline.

During this confrontation, Savathun revealed to her sister the truth, that they had all been played by the Witness. Savathun explained the ramifications of a "war to end all wars", and how the Witness's victory would ultimately be a loss for the Hive's God of War. And then she explained the truth of their past, that it had been using and manipulating the Hive the whole time, that its betrayal is ultimately what led Crota to Earth, what caused Oryx to fall. Xivu was furious—she could tell her sister was being honest. And thus, on the eve of the Final Shape, the entire Hive Pantheon turned on the Witness, begrudgingly allying with the forces of the Traveler (but for only one fight).

The war council was tense. Caiatl personally challenged Xivu Arath to a fight once the Witness was dealt with, Zavala had his hands full trying to keep a brawl from starting, Crow was off patrolling with Cayde, Ikora was convening with her Hidden, Savathun was nonchalant but backed her sister (she knew that Xivu was going to follow through with her own plans, regardless of if everyone else agreed, so she was trying to keep a united front), Mithrax was clearly uncomfortable and wanted to end it quickly, and Mara was trying to maintain a sense of reason throughout it. Mara proposed a strike force to scale the Monolith, followed by the full strength of their armies in a follow-up attack, backed by Mithrax. Xivu Arath, however, disagreed. Her proposal would see all their forces trying to scale the Monolith. Ultimately, Xivu's direct approach was reluctantly agreed to, primarily due to Savathun's influence on her sister's behalf.

The combined forces of the Hive pantheon, the Awoken, the House of Light, the Cabal Empire, the Guardians, and the rest of humanity's allies charged the Monolith. Many died along the way, and the fight at the top was even more brutal. The Guardian led the charge. In a final, last-ditch effort to stop the Witness at its most powerful, their Ghost channeled the Traveler's Light into a powerful, weaponized beam, blasting the Witness, ending the Witness once and for all. This energy was far too much for the Ghost—its shell shattered with the exertion, its core clattering lifelessly to the ground. The damage was too severe, the Traveler too weakened—there was no saving the Little Light in this timeline.

After the Witness's death, the now-Lightless Guardian was assigned to Tower duty to serve as a teacher for Light- and Dark-bearers alike, including Zavala. With their expertise, the Vanguard did not want to risk them in the field—and the Guardian took to this job with as much vigor as they could muster. The loss of their companion had left a lasting injury. And so, when the Echoes of The Witness were discovered, it was up to new, different groups of Guardians to deal with them.

One Echo has fallen into the hands of the Vex. This was confirmed with utmost urgency by the arrival of a Harpy unit in the Tower—a messenger from within the Vex Net. It relayed the Vex's plans, as well as the location of the Echo and the machine it was being used to power.

"The Vex are planning to use the Echo to power a paracausal engine, a previously-theoretical Vex device that would create a stable unidirectional bridge to reach into alternate timelines and pull paracausal energy into ours; this would alter the fundamental nature of our timeline, creating a paradox of sorts, which the engine would then force-correct using the excess paracausal energy. Paracausal energy is like a battery; it all exists in one form or another in the circuits of our reality; the engine would essentially create new energy, free from the tethers of our reality, that could be used for whatever the Vex wish. The end result would be limitless amounts of paracausal energy, and something in the Vex Network has clearly used the Echo to begin to understand the nature of paracausality."

If this Echo is allowed to remain in Vex hands, they will eventually complete the paradox engine, uncover the secrets of paracausality, and finally have the means to create their perfect timeline, however they see fit. You are the fireteam being given this task. You must either scout out the situation and report back or shut it down if you can, reclaiming the Echo in the process.
